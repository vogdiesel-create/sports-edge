# Morning Analysis - April 18, 2026

## Critical Bug Found and Fixed

### Grading Bug: Cross-Day Score Matching
**Severity: HIGH** - Was corrupting the paper ledger with false results.

The `grade_bets()` function in `paper_trader.py` fetched scores from multiple dates and stored them in a flat dict keyed by team names (e.g., `"Baltimore Orioles @ Cleveland Guardians"`). When the same teams play on consecutive days (common in MLB series), the second day's score overwrites the first. Today's April 18 picks were logged at 11:18 UTC and graded at 11:20 UTC against stale/wrong scores.

**Affected bets (reverted to pending):**
- BAL @ CLE UNDER 8.0: was graded as LOSS (actual_total=10) - game hasn't been played
- MIL @ MIA UNDER 8.0: was graded as LOSS (actual_total=12) - game hasn't been played
- CIN @ MIN UNDER 8.5: was graded as WIN (actual_total=3) - game hasn't been played
- LAD @ COL ML AWAY: was graded as WIN (actual_total=8) - game hasn't been played

**Fix:** Changed score storage from `{game_key: score}` to `{date: {game_key: score}}` and modified the grading loop to only match bets against scores from the bet's own date.

## Analysis

### What's Working
1. **K-prop model is the star.** 72% WR, 12.3% ROI, and consistent. UNDER picks are particularly strong (4W-1L, +$267.39).
2. **Totals model is profitable.** 8.7% ROI is solid for a small sample. The UNDER bias approach is validated.
3. **Combined +$468.48** is real money. The system works when we stick to what's profitable.

### What's Not Working
1. **Moneylines are a disaster.** AWAY 0-6 despite backtest. Now disabled.
2. **CLV is negative.** Average -0.35%, only 39% positive. This means the closing line moves AGAINST us more often than not. We're not consistently beating the market's final number.
3. **Repeat matchup bias.** BAL@CLE bet 3 times in 3 days, MIL@MIA bet 3 times. The model doesn't account for series context where information gets priced in after game 1.

### Highest-Impact Improvement Today
**Investigate repeat matchup bias.** The model betting the same matchup 3 days in a row is suspicious. By game 2-3 of a series, the market has adjusted to the specific pitching/goalie matchups. Our model may be using stale season-level stats that don't account for this. If we're losing on repeat bets, we should add a "series game number" penalty or skip games where we've already bet the same matchup.

### CLV Deep Dive Needed
The -0.35% average CLV is concerning. Possible explanations:
1. We're betting too early (lines haven't settled)
2. We're betting stale odds (model uses snapshot, not real-time)
3. Our model is systematically less accurate than the market
4. Mix of all three

Need to break down CLV by: time of placement, sport, edge tier, and win/loss.
