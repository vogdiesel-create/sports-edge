## The Three Big Findings This Week

### 1. CLV Separates Winners from Losers (32pp spread)

| CLV Status | Record | Win Rate | PnL |
|-----------|--------|----------|-----|
| Positive CLV | 10W-3L | 76.9% | +$914 |
| Negative CLV | 9W-11L | 45.0% | -$189 |

When we beat the closing line, we win ~77% of the time. When we don't, it's a coin flip. Average CLV is -0.42%, meaning most bets are placed behind the market. **If we could predict which bets will have positive CLV and filter accordingly, the model becomes dramatically more profitable.**

### 2. Edge Sweet Spot is 8-12% (not 5-12%)

| Edge Range | Record | PnL | WR |
|-----------|--------|-----|-----|
| 5-8% | 7W-8L | +$12 | 47% |
| 8-10% | 3W-2L-1P | +$122 | 60% |
| 10-12% | 6W-2L | +$521 | 75% |
| 12%+ | 5W-7L | -$399 | 42% |

The 5-8% tier is basically break-even. All the profit comes from 8-12%. Consider raising the minimum edge from 5% to 8% for totals, though this reduces volume significantly.

### 3. K-Prop Model Overestimates Ks by +0.58

The model's systematic bias explains everything about K-prop performance:
- **UNDER**: Model thinks UNDER is less likely than it actually is -> conservative probabilities -> real edge exists -> 75% WR
- **OVER**: Model thinks OVER is more likely than it actually is -> inflated probabilities -> false edges -> 58% WR

Fix: Either apply a -0.58 K calibration offset or raise the OVER minimum edge to 8%+ to compensate.

## What's Not Working

1. **CLV is net negative** (-0.42%). We're consistently behind the market. Either we're betting too early (before lines settle) or our odds snapshots are stale.
2. **K-prop OVERs** are unprofitable (-$164). The model's K overestimation makes OVER bets systematically flawed.
3. **5-8% edge bets** are essentially random (47% WR). We're generating lots of noise in this range.
4. **Repeat matchup bias** - Betting the same series matchup 3 days in a row (BAL@CLE, MIL@MIA) doesn't account for market adjustment.
